## 1. Merge Rules Applied

### 1.1 Cascade Consolidation

| Merged Into | Sources | Rationale |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **C-01 Leverage Cascade** | Margin spiral (Great Depression), Repo cascade (GFC), Leverage-liquidity spiral (GFC), Extraction-depletion spiral (Roman) | All share structure: initial shock → forced liquidation → price decline → more liquidation. Unified under "leverage" as common enabling factor. |
| **C-02 Confidence Contagion** | Bank run contagion, Counterparty contagion, Institutional erosion cascade, Confidence collapse | All share structure: trust loss → preemptive withdrawal → actual failure. Distinguished from C-01 by absence of mechanical forcing (leverage) and presence of belief dynamics. |
| **C-03 Retaliation Cascade** | Trade war spiral, Sanctions cascade, Tariff retaliation, Diplomatic escalation | All share structure: unilateral action → symmetric response → escalation. Distinguished from other cascades by intentional (not automatic) response. |

### 1.2 Transformation Consolidation

| Merged Into | Sources | Rationale |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **T-01 Institutional Substitution** | Functional displacement (Roman), Gatekeeper replacement (Printing), Shadow institution (various) | All share gradual function transfer without formal handover. Distinguished from T-03 (co-optation) by absence of explicit alliance. |
| **T-02 Procedural Transformation** | Voting rule changes, Committee restructuring, Policy paradigm shifts | All share power shift through rule change rather than confrontation. Unified under "procedural" as common lever. |
| **T-03 State Co-optation** | Establishment incorporation, Official adoption, Mainstreaming | All share movement-state alliance trading autonomy for resources. Distinguished from T-01 by explicit (not gradual) alliance. |

### 1.3 Feedback Consolidation

| Merged Into | Sources | Rationale |
|-------------|---------|-----------|

| **F-02 Network Effect Amplification** | Metcalfe effects, Bandwagon dynamics, Knowledge network effects | All share superlinear value scaling with users. Distinguished from other feedback by network structure requirement. |
| **F-03 Debt-Deflation Spiral** | Fisher spiral, Balance sheet recession, Real debt amplification | Kept specific rather than generalizing to "deflation effects" because the debt-price interaction is the distinctive mechanism. |

### 1.4 Vulnerability Consolidation

## 2. Rejected Patterns

### 2.1 Rejected as Thematic (Not Mechanistic)

| Pattern | Domain | Rejection Rationale |
|---------|--------|---------------------|
| Hubris-nemesis | Philosophy | Describes outcome pattern, not causal mechanism. No operational definition of when hubris triggers nemesis. |
| Golden age decline | History | Narrative frame, not mechanism. Multiple competing mechanisms produce "decline" outcomes. |
| Prophetic succession | Religion | Describes role transfer, but mechanism varies (hereditary, charismatic, institutional). |
| Creative destruction | Economics | Schumpeterian label for outcome; actual mechanisms are Gatekeeper Displacement, Reproduction Cost Collapse, etc. |

### 2.2 Rejected as Biographical (Person-Specific)

| Pattern | Domain | Rejection Rationale |

| Medici feedback loop | Art | Specific case of MP-06 Competitive Patronage Escalation + capital-legitimacy conversion. |

## 3. Known Ambiguities (Watch for v0.2)

### 3.1 May Require Splitting

| Current Pattern | Ambiguity | Resolution Criteria |
|-----------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **C-02 Confidence Contagion** | Currently covers both rational (information cascade) and irrational (pure panic) contagion. May need split. | If evidence shows different intervention points, split into "Information Cascade" and "Panic Contagion." |
| **T-01 Institutional Substitution** | Covers both competitive displacement and cooperative succession. May need split. | If evidence shows different dynamics, split into "Competitive Substitution" and "Cooperative Succession." |
| **MP-08 Counter-Reformation Lag** | ~100 year lag is empirical observation from limited cases. May not generalize. | Track lag duration across new cases. If variance exceeds 50 years, may need parameterization or split. |
| **V-04 Risk Opacity Amplification** | Currently covers both inadvertent opacity (supply chains) and designed opacity (financial engineering). May need split. | If evidence shows different intervention points, split into "Inadvertent Opacity" and "Designed Opacity." |

### 3.2 May Require Merging

| Candidates | Overlap | Resolution Criteria |
|------------|---------|---------------------|
| **V-02 Policy Constraint Lock-in** + **MP-02 Threshold Transition** | Lock-in may be pre-condition for threshold transition when it finally breaks. | If evidence shows consistent co-occurrence, consider merging into "Lock-in-then-Break" pattern. |
| **T-03 State Co-optation** + **MP-12 Network Parasitism** | Co-optation may be special case of parasitism (movement parasitizes state). | If evidence shows common mechanism, consider merging. |

### 3.3 Boundary Cases Requiring Clarification

| Pattern | Boundary Case | Current Ruling | Rationale |
|---------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| **C-01 Leverage Cascade** | Does tax extraction (Roman) count as "leverage"? | Yes | Extraction-to-capacity ratio functions like leverage ratio; forced liquidation equivalent is depopulation/abandonment. |
| **F-01 Accountability Feedback Loop** | Does internal performance review count? | No | Require external evaluation; internal monitoring is different mechanism. |
| **PO-01 Atomization-Capture** | Does social media isolation count as "atomization"? | Provisionally yes | Digital isolation may differ structurally from physical isolation; watch for evidence. |
